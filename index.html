About Fruit Colour

Noun: colour: a phenomenon of light (such as red, brown, pink, or gray) or visual perception that enables one to differentiate otherwise identical objects

Fruit comes in all sorts of colos. The green found in many fruits, such as limes, green apples and green grapes comes from the life giving clorophil that can be found in every plant under the sun. Other colours, such as reds and yellows give hints as to the vitamins avaliable in the fruit. Fruit uses it's sugars and bright colours to attract creatures to spread it's seeds. These colours biologically appeal to our eyes by advritizing the vitamins the morsel has to offer. colour also plays an important role in the identifiation of the ripeness of fruit. Fruits such as peppers go beyond colour to help spread it's fruit, since mammals digenst pepper seeds, the fruit tastes spicy and hurts them, but birds, who don't destroy the seeds in their digestive track can't taste the spice, thus allowing more pepper plants to grow.
